5 Reasons to Use Resume Action Words

  1. They’re shorter. Since your resume should be condensed to one page, this means that the information under each employment position needs to say a lot in as few words as possible. Resume action words can help you do this.
  2. They’re more specific. The role of your resume is to paint a picture of the type of employee that you can be. You can do this by using resume action words to make your bullet points more specific.
  3. They’re less cliché. With lots of people applying for the same job, you want your resume to stand out. Using purposeful words will help make your resume more unique, helping you outshine the competition.
  4. They increase scannability. Before your resume ends up before the hiring manager, most companies use an electronic short-listing process to whittle it down to the best candidates. In order to bypass this process, you should look for important resume action words from the job description.
  5. They have a greater impact. One of the best reasons to use resume action words is that they leave a lasting impact.

How to Avoid Redundancy

  • Elaborate the most on more recent positions. While you may have held a similar role at a few companies, focus on your more recent accomplishments.
  • Focus on results. If you have assumed the same responsibilities in various roles, aim to differentiate between them.
  • Change up the wording. Many job seekers fall into the trap of using the same words over and over again to describe their experience. Make your resume stand out among the competition and pack a stronger punch by integrating strong action verbs.
  • Sell yourself. Make sure that each statement serves a purpose and positions you in a positive light. Grab attention by using powerful verbs and adjectives. Creating this strong impression can help you to avoid redundancy by carefully choosing the words and phrases that you use.

Why Quantifiable Impact

  • Avoid mentioning vague accomplishments. Hiring Managers would like to see quantifiable achievements which are getting highlighted in your resume. Hence ensure that you are using number and metrics (wherever relevant to quantify your achievements)


TENSES -Ensure that you are using the right tenses when describing your accomplishment

Why Use Correct Tenses

  1. Choose past tense when talking about the roles that you held but no longer do
  2. Use the present tense to explain what you do currently. These Include Activities you do at your occupation, Any tasks you engage in after your daily job routine, Volunteering gigs you participate in often, All other relevant tasks you are involved in.
  3. If you have to use the past and the present tense on your resume, do not mix them. Use the present to talk about your responsibilities and the past to denote your accomplishments and finished undertakings. And note, when updating your resume with recent roles, be sure to use past tense for your previous ones. As for your current job-related pursuits, be sure to use the present.

Conciseness - How to improve ?


Bullet points -

  1. Resume should predominantly contain short bullet points rather than paragraphs. If a recruiter was to pick up your resume right now, he should be able to skim through it in 30 seconds and your resume needs to be optimized for it. Short, succinct bullet points are key to your resume's readability. Blobs of text, that is bullets or paragraphs that are three lines or longer, are likely to not be read. With the exception of an optional 'Summary' section, you should be consistently using bullet pointsthroughout your resume.
  2. Too many bullet points – recruiters should get an understanding of your resume in 30 seconds. It is recommended that you should list between 10 – 18 bullet points in your resume. Less than 10 might mean that you do not have enough relevant experience and more than 20 means that you have included non – relevant accomplishments in your resume which may be overshadowing your actual achievements. Aim for 4 – 6 bullet points per experience.
  3. Please note that the bullet points are considered in only in the following section (ork Experience, Leadership, Projects and Extra-curriculars). Sections like obkectives, skills and summary sections are not included.
  4. Length – each bullet point should not be more that 3 lines long.
