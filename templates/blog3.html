Wheel of Life

If you are out to change your life, then the first thing you will need to do is to find out that what is not working for you and in your life. Your change will be more effective when you find out the exact problem and work towards managing that situation. But, how to do that?

In the current day and age with a full-time work schedule, it can be challenging to find a balance between your professional and personal responsibilities. there are many tools available to identify the same however, one of the most effective tools is The Wheel of Life.

This is a visual tool which has been designed to help you understand that currently how balanced is your life. This is an effective tool being used by many working professionals to visualize all the important areas of their life at once. You will be able to understand that which areas of your life are thriving and which areas need attention by getting a visual representation of all the critical areas of your life. The wheel points out those areas in which you might want something to be different or want some more happiness, success or change.

It can be a great way to establish goals and then regularly use to check-in about the progress of those agreed goals. The Wheel of Life is used to identify how you use your time, where your focus should be and how satisfied you feel right now. This tool facilitates self-reflection and so promotes the setting of good SMART goals, in order to get life moving in the right direction.

There are 8 categories which are arranged in a circular manner on a chart, hence the categories can be identified as spokes and the circle becomes the wheel. The 8 categories can be defined as:-

  • 1. Career – is focused on the addressing your satisfaction level in the area of what do you do for a living.
  • 2. Finance – is focused on the addressing your satisfaction level in the area of what do you do for a living.
  • 3. Personal Growth – is focused on the addressing your satisfaction level in the area of what do you do for a living.
  • 4. Health – this section refers to the way your feel about yourself from an mental and physical health perspective.
  • 5. Family and Friends / Social environment – refers to the level of satisfaction you have with your family / friends / relations etc.
  • 6. Love and Relations – refers to the level of satisfaction you have with your loved ones, partner, spouse.
  • 7. Fun and Recreation – refers to your level of satisfaction towards enjoyment, following your hobbies, recreational activities, etc.
  • 8. Society – measures your level of involvement on social causes, communities, etc.

Approach

Click on the link to access the Wheel of Life template. The eight categories have been mentioned on the sheet. Refer to the questions which have been mentioned below each sections before giving yourself a score on a scale of 0 to 10 (0 being lowest and 10 being highest).

Interpretation of the scores:

  • • Scores between 8 – 10 – you are very satisfied in that life area.

  • • Scores between 5 – 7 –you are fairly satisfied in that life area. There is some room for improvement.

  • • Scores between 1 – 4 –it is clear that you are not very satisfied in that life area. You need to ask yourself that what can you do about this? What SMART goals can be put in place to take this to the next level.

    • Once you have finished allocating the scores, you will see a spider graph which appears by connecting all the dots as per the scores which you have given for each category. This will give you a view about how each area compares to each other and you get to decide that whether the wheel looks balanced for you or not. Different areas of your life require different amounts of attention, specifically in various seasons. In other words, achieving a balanced life doesn't have to mean that you're giving an equal amount of focus to each category.

    You can now think and consider the Ideal level for each category and map the same on the graph. This will help you in identifying the gaps between your current and ideal levels thus leading towards creating focused SMART goals for reducing the categories with the major gaps. There could be areas where you feel you're unable to devote your desired amount of attention because you're too focused on another dimension. Using this visual tool, you can identify the gaps and decide what you need to do to establish balance.

    The Wheel of Life is all about identifying the probable areas of for change and identifying your ideal balance. Do not get downhearted by the low scores, instead start focusing on creating some realistic SMART goals to support yourself. Spend time in some introspection or speak to a trusted confidante or a friend.

    Better still, you can connect for a session with one of our coaches and have a discussion about what is going on in your mind and how would you want to go about creating your SMART goals.
